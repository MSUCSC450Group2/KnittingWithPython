Measuring the gauge

For knitting and crochet

1. Knit/crochet a swatch using pattern, yarn and needles/hook you will use for your project.
2. Make a swatch that is at least 20 stitches in width and 15 rows in height. The larger the swatch, the more accurate the measurements.
3. To get the most accurate gauge measurement, wash the swatch, lay it flat and let it dry completely before you measure the gauge.
4. Take a measuring tape or ruler and measure the width of some number of inner stitches (Picture 1) and the height of some number of inner rows (Picture 2).
5. Calculate the gauge size using our calculator.
